Schooling and stuff

I did odyssey of the mind and read books until middle school. In middle school and highschool I played soccer and messed around with computers. I graduated from The Hill Family Boarding School but spent 11 years at Owen J. Roberts. I transfered because I had the oppurtunity to. I've taken up climbing but enjoy skiing and a good bike/run/swim. I'd like to do a triathalon but I also just like activities involving those events. I have this thing with writing where I've avoided it and now I have spurts where I love to hear my fingers tap a keyboard and wrist ache from scrawling paper. Work

I started work at an autobody shop. My grandmother gave me her old wagon before I could drive so I worked a summer at an autobody shop to get my new car painted. I wanted chrome blue highlights on a matte black body. This guy talked me into white with silver highlights. Then I went to work for a local golf club. I took care of the golf carts on one course and picked the range on two. Eventually I caddied mornings there before other work. I dabbled with MLM selling cutco knives. An honestly decent to good product, for some even great. I sold thousands in knives and saw a couple hundred bucks for exploiting family and friends. I felt hassled by the boss so I got a job bussing tables at the nicest french restaurant I've ever seen. I'll book table 3 for an anniversey one day (its like a $100 a meal, it's not a casual thing :/) Then I worked for a crypto tax guy for a while. Real internship type thing at a small company. Learned a lot about taxes, tokens vs coins, work vs stake, and small business operations. I then went on to work in the strictest little liquor store just off the University campus. It was a lot of fun and I was good at spotting fake ID's so I quickly got promoted and ended up running a small retail store in the fall of COVID. I learned the back-end at the tax firm and I learned the front-end at the liquor store. After the liquor store I decided to not work or volunteer for my last semester but I ended up working in a pub kitchen as a line cook. A busy afternoon would call for something like 5 bodies. We would have 3 and the other 2 would be out back smoking leaving me to do it all since I don't smoke. It was fun but I'd never been disrespected by management before which really drove me to pursue my career. Throughout this whole working endeaver I've been around some live events. I've done odd jobs like scan tickets or wrap cable. I've also spent a little time around AV equipment so I know some terms and procedures. Just enough to know I don't know much, maybe a tiny bit more. I've ran cameras for a few concerts and festivals and even some tour rehearsals. Long lens, in the pit, give me some headphones and I'll get what the director wants. Then I studied disguise media servers for the 4th time and finally went to a show to see how it all actually works. It's a lot of cool and I have some crazy sets for some artists I like and I can program these shows and know what it takes to run the whole thing. DOWNLOAD MY RESUME BELOW
